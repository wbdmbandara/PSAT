Suspicious sender

Always verify the sender address and domain before trusting the message.

Urgent language

Attackers often create urgency to reduce careful thinking and force quick action.

Unverified links

How to report a phishing email

1. Do not click again

Stop interacting with the message once something feels off.

2. Report it to IT or Security

Use your organization's phishing-report button or send it to your security mailbox.

3. Delete the message

After reporting it, remove it from your inbox to avoid accidental clicks.

Good habits to remember

  • Pause before acting on urgent requests.
  • Double-check domain names and login pages.
  • Never share passwords through email.
  • When in doubt, report instead of replying.
